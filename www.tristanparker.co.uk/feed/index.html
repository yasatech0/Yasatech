]]> SEO (or Search Engine Optimisation) is absolutely essential for successful websites that want to attract a healthy flow of traffic. However, how long does SEO take to work in 2022? Of course, immediate results would be preferred, yet this is unlikely – so what timeframe can you expect after the implementation of search engine optimisation tools? Read on to find out. 

How long to see SEO results?

Typically SEO can take anywhere from 30 days to 12 months to work noticeably, but on average, you can expect between four to six months to pass before you start seeing any results. This will vary based on a number of factors, including your website’s age, your website’s past history with search engines, and whether or not your website has received any search engine penalties before. 

Even the best executed SEO campaigns take time to show results for your website, and this may cause you to begin questioning whether your campaign is actually working. Just because your web traffic doesn’t explode within a few days or weeks, this doesn’t mean that your SEO efforts have gone unnoticed, as the long term effects of a top quality SEO campaign are most certainly worth the wait. 

So, How Long Does SEO Take For A New Business? 

Similar to a young adult seeking their first credit card, a new business will likely have little probable history that can be used to certify their authenticity and value. This can mean that any SEO efforts that you implement for your new business website may take a lot longer to impact on your traffic, as the search engine will need far more evidence to ascertain your reliability and worth online. It can usually take more than 6 months to a year for a search engine to properly index a new site, but thankfully you can speed up the process by getting links from other established high quality websites. This essentially acts as a reference, as a high quality site linking to your own proves to your search engine that you are a worthwhile platform. 

How Long Does It Take To Get Organic Traffic? 

Organic traffic describes visits to your website coming from a search engine’s organic results, and not as a result of paid ads. Organic traffic is definitely the most important form of traffic that you can get to your website. 100 unique organic visits daily in almost any niche topic can be acquired in about 3 to 4 months, but only if your marketing and advertising campaigns are strong enough to attract sufficient attention to your business and website. 

Is SEO Better Than PPC? 

The most notable difference between organic SEO traffic and PPC (pay per click) is that organic traffic is free, whereas PPC is paid for. With SEO you can get traffic without any kind of cost or bill 24/7, but as previously discussed it is not instant – it can take time for SEO to generate serious results. Comparatively, with PPC advertising you can benefit from much faster results, but you then need to pay every time someone clicks on your adverts to visit your website.

]]> In today’s digital age, it’s more important than ever to have a well-designed website. A web designer can help take your business to the next level by creating an attractive and user-friendly website that will engage your customers and keep them coming back for more. So, what do web designers do? And when is the right time to use one?

What Is A Web Designer?

A web designer is someone who creates the look and feel of a website. They work with graphics, typography, and layout to create a visually appealing and user-friendly design. They make the overall design of the website and its pages, and they decide how the content will be arranged. Web designers also create graphics and logos for websites, and they may be responsible for coding HTML and CSS. A good web designer can help you achieve your business goals by creating an effective online presence that meets your unique needs.

What Do Web Designers Do?

Understanding what web designers do can be tricky for someone who does not understand the terminology; however broken down into simple English it’s easy to understand! Web designers are responsible for the look, feel, and functionality of a website. They work with clients to understand their needs and then create a design that meets those needs. Web designers must be able to translate complex ideas into easy-to-understand visuals, and they must have a strong understanding of web development principles.

How Can You Tell If You Need A Web Designer?

If you’re not sure whether or not you need a web designer, ask yourself the following questions:

  • Does my website look dated or outdated?
  • Do I have a lot of content that needs to be organised and structured in a way that is user friendly?
  • Am I having difficulty creating pages or adding new content?
  • Is my website not performing well in search engine results?

If you answered yes to any of these questions, it’s likely that you could benefit from working with a web designer. There are several situations when it’s appropriate to hire a web designer. For example, if you have an outdated website or find it challenging to update, if you need help organising your website content, or if you want to improve your website’s search engine ranking, it’s time to hire a web designer. Additionally, if you are launching a new business or redesigning an existing business website, it’s typically best to work with a professional web designer.

What Does A Freelance Web Designer Do?

As a freelance web designer you will work with clients to create a website that meets their specific needs. You will take their brief and design a visually appealing and user-friendly website. A good web designer will also ensure that the website is optimised for search engines so that it can be found by potential customers online.

If you are looking for a bespoke website design, then a freelance web designer is the perfect choice for you. They will work with you one-on-one to create a unique website that reflects your brand and personality. However, if you are on a tight budget or only need a simple website design, it might be worth considering using a template instead of hiring a designer.

Template websites are pre-designed websites that you can buy or download for free. They are a great option if you want a website quickly and don’t have the time or budget to hire a designer. However, if you need a customised website that is unique to your business, then a template will not be suitable. In this case, it is best to hire a freelance web designer to create a bespoke design for you.

How To Become A Web Designer?

There are several ways to become a web designer. You can choose one of the following ways to obtain the necessary experience:

  • A college and obtain a Certificate or Diploma in Web Design.
  • A university and pursue a bachelor’s degree (B.Sc in Multimedia and Web Design), or you could further your studies and complete a masters degree (M.Sc in E-commerce and Web Design).
  • Or you could complete a couple of online courses which will provide you with the experience and knowledge you need to pursue a career in Web Design.

No matter which route you take, it’s crucial to have a good understanding of the basics of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. These are the three most commonly used programming languages in web design. Additionally, it’s also essential to have strong graphic design skills, as you will be working with visuals a lot.

What Skills Do You Need For Web Design?

Web design is a very versatile field that can be used for various purposes, from creating an online portfolio to designing a website for your business. To become a web designer, you must understand what web designers do. You need to have some basic skills in graphic design and experience in coding and programming languages. You don’t necessarily need to know how to code from scratch, but it is helpful to understand the basics so you can communicate better with other developers.

The skills needed are:

  • Creativity
  • Technical ability
  • Attention to detail
  • Communication skills
  • Outstanding IT skills
  • Imagination
  • Patience
  • Analytical skills

Another essential skill for web designers is user experience (UX) design. This involves understanding how people interact with websites and ensuring the layout and navigation are easy to use. As more and more people use their mobile devices to access the internet, it’s becoming increasingly important for web designers to be familiar with responsive design principles.

If you want to work as a freelance web designer, you will also need to be able to market yourself and sell your services to clients. You can do this by creating a portfolio of your previous work, setting up a website or blog, and networking with other professionals in the industry.

Can I Learn Web Design Myself? 

Some people can teach themselves the basics of web design, while others need more guidance and direction in order to learn effectively. You need to decide what would work best for you. If you’re serious about learning web design, there are plenty of online resources and tutorials that can help you get started. However, if you want or need a more structured learning environment, it may be worth considering taking a course or attending a workshop.

When deciding whether or not to learn web design yourself, consider your motivations and goals. For example, do you want to build simple websites for your own personal use? Or do you aspire to become a professional web designer? If you’re looking for a career change and want to become self-employed, web design may be a good option for you. But if you’re just looking to create a basic website for your business, you may not need to learn all the intricacies of web design.

]]> When you are thinking of creating a website, the first question that comes to mind is how much it will cost. The answer to this question can vary greatly depending on the features and expectations you have for your website. In this article, we will discuss some of the factors that contribute to the cost of a website. We will also offer some tips on keeping your costs down without sacrificing quality.

Website Cost 

When it comes to website design, the cost is always a concern. How much will it set me back? How much do I need to spend to have a great website? How much should I budget for my website?

These are all valid questions, and, unfortunately, there is no easy answer. The cost of a website depends on various factors, including the features you want, the design and development time required, your hosting fees, and any potential management costs.

In general, though, you can expect to pay anywhere between £500 and £2000 for a simple brochure website, while e-commerce or interactive sites can cost as much as tens of thousands.

Different Features

When it comes to creating a website, many different features can be included. These features include a custom design, eCommerce capabilities, search engine optimization, social media integration, and more. The cost of these features will vary depending on the company you work with. For example, a custom design may cost more than integrating social media into your site.

Expectations

When creating a website, it’s essential to have clear expectations for what you want the site to do. Are you looking to create a simple brochure-style website or an eCommerce store? Each type of website requires different features and will cost differently.

E-commerce vs. Brochure Sites

There is a big difference between e-commerce and brochure sites when it comes to website costs.

  • An e-commerce site needs an online store, payment gateway, shipping integration, and more to enable customers to buy products and services online.
  • A brochure website is just that – a website with information about your company and its products or services.

Hosting Fees

The cost of hosting a website will depend on the size of your site, the hosting you choose, and any additional features you need, such as SSL certification or domain name registration.

Hosting fees can vary greatly depending on the plan you choose. Most web hosting companies will offer a range of plans, starting with a basic shared hosting plan and moving up through VPS, dedicated server and cloud hosting options. However, the most expensive option doesn’t necessarily mean it’s the best for your business needs – you need to carefully consider the best hosting plan for your business.

Potential Management Costs

In addition to the cost of creating and hosting a website, you may also need to pay for website management. This includes updating content, adding new products or services, monitoring site performance, and more. Website management costs vary depending on the size and complexity of your website, your requirements, and the amount of maintenance work you want to do yourself. If you choose a website management package that includes all the tasks above, it will usually be more expensive than if you don’t need those services. Generally speaking, larger websites cost more to manage than smaller ones because they have more content and features.

]]> When it comes to online marketing, there are many options to choose from. Two of the most popular are PPC and SEO. However, both have pros and cons, making it difficult to decide which one is right for your business. In this article, we will discuss the pros and cons of both PPC and SEO so that you can make an informed decision about which one is right for you.

Is PPC Better Than SEO?

When it comes to online marketing, there are many options to choose from. Two of the most common are PPC and SEO. Both have pros and cons, so how do you decide which is better for your business?

PPC involves paying for ads that appear in search engines. When someone clicks on your ad, you pay a predetermined amount, typically determined by how competitive your industry is. On the other hand, SEO is the process of optimising your website and content to rank higher in search engine results pages (SERPs).

What Are The Key Differences Between SEO and PPC?

The main difference between SEO and PPC is that with SEO, your site will rank higher in unpaid search engine results pages (SERPs), while with PPC, you’ll pay for each time someone clicks on your ad. Here are some other key differences:

  • PPC is paid advertising, while SEO is unpaid. PPC shows up at the top of search results pages and can be a great way to get traffic right away, but it’s expensive. With SEO, you optimise your website for search engines so that you rank higher in SERPs. This means more organic (i.e., free) traffic to your site, but it takes more time and effort than PPC.
  • PPC can be turned on or off at any time, while SEO requires continuous work to keep your website ranking high in SERPs. With PPC, you stop paying for ads when you turn them off; with SEO, the process is much more involved. You might have to go back and change your website’s code, content, or structure if Google were to decide that the elements you used when optimising your site are no longer relevant.
  • SEO can help optimise all of your pages, while PPC focuses on one landing page at a time. With SEO, you want to make sure all of your pages are optimised so that you can get as much traffic as possible. With PPC, you only need to optimise the page where people will click on your ad.
  • SEO is long-term, while PPC is short-term. SEO takes time and effort but lasts for a long time, while PPC gets you traffic quickly but can be expensive.

Pros and Cons of SEO

Pros:

  • SEO is free organic process.
  • SEO can help you get more website traffic over time.
  • SEO can help you rank higher in SERPs for relevant keywords.
  • You only need to optimise your website once, and it will continue to work long term.

Cons:

  • It takes a while to rank higher in SERPs, typically 3-12 months depending on keyword difficulty.
  • Google is constantly updating its algorithm, so you might have to go back and update your site’s code as well as its content regularly.

Pros and Cons of PPC

Pros:

  • PPC is an effective way to get traffic quickly.
  • You can target your ads to specific audiences.
  • PPC is an excellent option for e-commerce websites.

Cons:

  • PPC can be expensive.
  • You need to continuously monitor your ads and make changes as needed.
  • PPC has a lower click-through rate than organic search results.

Is SEO a PPC?

SEO is not a type of PPC. SEO is unpaid, while the advertiser must pay for PPC.

Is PPC Cheaper Than SEO?

PPC ads can be cheaper than SEO. While PPC may cost more in the short term, it doesn’t have to be expensive in the long run if you know how to manage your budget and bids effectively. You can also save money with PPC by turning off your ads when they’re no longer relevant or useful.

SEO vs.Google Ads

In conclusion, considering PPC vs. SEO. While SEO and Google Ads offer different benefits, they are both essential aspects of online marketing. When deciding which one to focus on for your business, you should consider your goals, budget, and the competition in your industry. For example, if you’re looking to generate more leads or sales through your website, PPC may be a better option. However, SEO is the better choice if you’re looking to improve your website’s visibility and organic search traffic.

SEO is a long-term strategy that takes time to see results, while PPC is a short-term strategy that can provide immediate results. If you have the budget, you may want to do both SEO and PPC simultaneously, so you’ll have more traffic coming from different sources.

In addition, if you want your business to rank higher on Google search results pages (SERPs), it would be best for you to focus on SEO first before doing any paid advertising campaigns. This will help build your organic search traffic, which is the most valuable type of traffic there is.

]]> SEO, or Search Engine Optimisation, is one of the most important aspects of online marketing for small businesses. When done correctly, it can help your website rank higher in Search Engine Results Pages (SERPs), which means more potential customers will see your business and be able to visit your website.

Here we will discuss some of the reasons why local SEO for small businesses is so important.

More People Are Using Search Engines To Find Businesses

In recent years, there has been a significant shift in the way people find businesses. A study found that about 45% of consumers who searched for something local on their phone visited a company within 24 hours. This means that if your business isn’t ranking high in SERPs, you’re likely missing out on a significant chunk of potential customers.

Local SEO for small business is essential because it allows you to target customers in your specific area. For example, if you own a restaurant in London, you would want to focus on optimising your website for searches related to “London restaurants” rather than just “restaurants.” This will help you to appear in results pages for people who are specifically looking for businesses in your area, rather than those who are just browsing.

Local SEO Is More Effective Than Other Forms Of Marketing

When it comes to online marketing, SEO is one of the most effective strategies you can use to generate more leads, calls or sales. Not only does it help you to reach more potential customers, but it’s also a very cost-effective way to advertise your business.

That’s because local SEO targets people who are already interested in what you have to offer. So when someone searches for a business like yours, they’re already looking for something specific, which means they’re more likely to be converted into a customer. Contrast this with traditional advertising methods such as TV or radio ads, which are seen and heard by a large number of people but only a tiny percentage of those people will be interested in what you’re selling.

It’s Easy To Set Up A Local SEO For Small Business

You can do a few things to set up a successful local SEO strategy. One of the easiest ways to do this is to ensure that your website is optimised for search engines. This means including keywords in your titles, headings, and descriptions and making sure that your site is easy to navigate. You should also create high-quality content that will attract links from other websites.
